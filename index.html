US Open

The US Open is one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments and takes place annually in New York City, typically from late August to early September. Played on hard courts, it is renowned for its fast-paced matches, energetic night sessions, and passionate crowds. Established in 1881, the tournament has evolved into a global spectacle, attracting the best players from around the world to compete in menâ€™s and womenâ€™s singles, doubles, and mixed doubles events.

With record-breaking prize money and millions of viewers worldwide, the US Open continues to be a defining force in the world of professional tennis.

French Open

The French Open, also known as Roland-Garros, is one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments and is held annually in Paris, France. Played on red clay courts, it is the only Grand Slam played on this unique surface, making it one of the most physically demanding tournaments in professional tennis. The slower surface and higher bounce require exceptional endurance, strategy, and skill, testing even the greatest players.


Established in 1891, the French Open has a rich history and is renowned for its passionate fans and iconic matches. The tournament typically takes place from late May to early June and attracts top players from around the world. The main stadium, Court Philippe Chatrier, is famous for its electric atmosphere during key matches. The French Open has seen legendary performances from players like Rafael Nadal, who holds the record for the most titles won at this event, and Serena Williams, who has also made her mark on the clay courts of Roland-Garros.

Australian Open

The Australian Open is the first Grand Slam of the tennis calendar year, held every January in Melbourne, Australia. Known for its vibrant energy, state-of-the-art facilities, and intense summer heat, it sets the stage for an exciting season of tennis. Played on hard courts at Melbourne Park, the tournament features men's and women's singles, doubles, mixed doubles, and junior events, attracting the world's top-ranked players.


Since its inception in 1905, the Australian Open has grown into a global sporting spectacle, earning the nickname "The Happy Slam" for its friendly atmosphere and fan engagement. With iconic venues like Rod Laver Arena and innovations such as retractable roofs and night matches, it offers an unmatched viewing experience.


Legends like Novak Djokovic, Serena Williams, and Roger Federer have all made history here, thrilling fans with unforgettable performances. The Australian Open combines elite competition with cultural flair, making it a must-watch event each year.
